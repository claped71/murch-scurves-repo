Rate Compression Summary — Root Cause: GameChange Delivery Delays
Supplier delivery failure is the single root cause of all downstream delays:

GC Lot 1 delivered piles, torque tubes, bearings, and some dampers — but zero usable purlins. Without purlins, tracker assembly is physically impossible regardless of pile completion status: modules clamp onto purlins, and purlins mount onto the torque tube — there is no installation step that can be reordered. All 186,585 usable purlins arrive in the 2nd GC batch (Jun 17→Jul 16), 25 CD past the committed finish (Jun 21). This single delivery gap cascades into a 36-CD tracker slip, 66-CD purlin slip, and 52-CD panel slip vs. contract baseline. Rate compression in Lot 2 (654/WD piles, 100/WD trackers) reflects recovery against supplier-caused lost time — not a productivity failure. Panels and purlins at 1.10–1.16× BL are achievable with adequate crew mobilization.
